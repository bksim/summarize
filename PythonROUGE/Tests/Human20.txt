Pfc. Bradley Manning, the soldier accused of providing diplomatic cables and other secret documents to the WikiLeaks website, pleaded guilty to 10 charges of misusing classified material on Thursday. Manning agreed to serve up to 20 years in prison as each charge carried a maximum 2-year sentence. Manning pleaded not guilty to 12 more serious charges, including espionage and aiding the enemy. The criminal case against Manning will go forward at a general court-martial in June. Manning faces a sentence of life in prison if convicted.
